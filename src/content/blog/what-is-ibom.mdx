# IBOM™ (the Intelligent Brand Operating Model) is an operating model for turning brand intent into **structured brand intelligence** that can be deployed into AI systems and governed over time.

## Why it exists
Most organisations already have brand strategy and guidelines, governance and risk functions, and teams delivering marketing, CX, data, and AI. What they often lack is a single operating model that connects:

- brand intent
- structured brand intelligence
- AI execution
- ongoing governance and assurance

When that connection is missing, brand behaviour in AI systems tends to be fragmented across teams and tools, informally encoded in prompts and playbooks, difficult to audit, and fragile under change.

![IBOM lifecycle: Assessment → Definition → Codification → Deployment → Operation → Assurance](/images/blog/what-is-ibom/ibom-lifecycle.svg)

<Insight>
IBOM™ is not a campaign system. It is a governance layer for brand execution across AI.
</Insight>

## What IBOM™ is (and is not)
IBOM™ defines *how* brand assets are created, deployed, executed, monitored, and evolved as an operational system.

- **It is:** a lifecycle with stages, controls, and responsibilities.
- **It is not:** a design system spec, a content calendar, or a set of one-off guidelines.

## Separation of concerns: definition vs operation
The Brando ecosystem is deliberately split into two layers:

- **Brando Schema** (definition layer): the linked-data vocabulary and Brand Definition Vocabulary (BDV) that encodes brand identity, expression, personas, context, and governance as data.
- **IBOM™** (operating layer): the lifecycle and discipline that governs how those Brando assets are introduced into systems, used at runtime, and assured over time.

Brando provides the building blocks. IBOM™ provides the operating discipline. Neither is sufficient on its own.

## The six IBOM™ stages
IBOM™ is designed to be repeatable. The same lifecycle can be applied to new brands, new markets, new AI use cases, or material changes in strategy and regulation.

### Assessment
**What brand intent, risk, and exposure must be understood?**

Assessment establishes the current state of brand readiness in an AI-driven environment. It identifies where brand meaning is ambiguous, where interpretation risk exists, and how AI systems may amplify misuse, inconsistency, or regulatory exposure. This stage surfaces priority AI use cases, evaluates potential value and risk, and produces initial AI Brand Impact Assessments. The goal is not to define the brand yet, but to understand what must be governed and why before formalisation begins.

### Definition
**How is brand intent formally defined in Brando terms?**

Definition translates implicit brand understanding into explicit, unambiguous intent. Brand values, narrative, tone, visual and behavioural rules are formally articulated, alongside constraints, exceptions, and approval logic. This stage creates a shared source of truth for what the brand means and permits, expressed in governance-ready language that can later be encoded. Definition resolves ambiguity and establishes the semantic foundation required for machine interpretation.

### Codification
**How is that intent encoded as machine-actionable intelligence?**

Codification converts defined brand intent into structured, executable data using the Brando Schema and Brand Definition Vocabulary (BDV). Brand meaning is expressed as a Brand Knowledge Graph, with context-aware personas, tokens, and policy derivation logic represented in formats such as JSON-LD or YAML. This is the point at which brand governance becomes computational: rules can be evaluated, policies inferred, and intent enforced automatically. This is where brand becomes executable.

### Deployment
**Where and how is it introduced into AI systems and workflows?**

Deployment activates codified brand intelligence within live systems. Versioned IBOM artefacts are introduced via APIs, runtime integrations, and controlled workflows aligned with enterprise architecture and security requirements. Validation and approval mechanisms ensure only authorised definitions reach production. Deployment is deliberate and controlled, ensuring brand governance is embedded into AI systems without disrupting operational stability.

### Operation
**How does it behave at runtime across channels and contexts?**

Operation is where brand governance functions continuously as a system of record. Brand policies are enforced at inference time across channels, tools, and use cases, with support for controlled overrides, campaigns, and contextual variation. Behaviour is monitored in real time, ensuring that AI outputs remain aligned with brand intent as they are generated. Integration is not a one-off task but an ongoing operational capability.

### Assurance
**How is compliance, quality, and drift monitored and corrected?**

Assurance ensures long-term trust, defensibility, and evolution of the brand operating model. Audit trails, traceability, and updated impact assessments provide evidence of compliance for internal and external stakeholders. Drift in behaviour or interpretation is detected and remediated, enabling continuous improvement as regulations, markets, or brand strategy evolve. Assurance closes the loop, ensuring the system remains accurate, accountable, and future-proof.

<Note>
IBOM™ is designed to be compatible with static-first publishing. Definitions can be versioned in-repo, reviewed through PR workflows, and deployed as controlled artefacts—without relying on a CMS.
</Note>
